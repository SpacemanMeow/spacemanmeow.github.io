In 2026, there are too many budget tracking and bookkeeping apps on the market, making it hard to choose the one that suits you best. Should you pick the most popular one? The one with the most features? Or the one that's truly useful and fun? To help you make a choice, we've objectively compared 5 popular budget tracking apps by analyzing their app store information and pros and cons to help you select based on your needs. We've also included Meow Money Manager for your reference. Let's start with the competitors (all are popular options on Google Play):

1. Money Manager Expense & Budget: Solid basic functions, supports income/expense tracking, budget management, and simple chart statistics. Adapts to basic bookkeeping needs. The interface is clean but the style is traditional and lacks personality. Doesn't support multiple ledgers and dark mode. Core functions are free, some advanced features require paid unlocking. No obvious ad interference.

2. Money Manager: Expense Tracker: Simple and easy to use, suitable for bookkeeping beginners. Supports income/expense categorization, budget reminders, and data backup functions. The interface is clean and intuitive, but there are ads in the app. Doesn't support dark mode and fingerprint lock. Privacy protection functions are weak. Advanced features require paid activation. No multiple ledger management.

3. Money manager & expenses: Focuses on basic financial management, supports income/expense recording, asset tracking, and simple statistics. Small memory footprint, runs smoothly, suitable for low-end devices. However, category icons are monotonous, lacks design sense. Doesn't support CSV export and cloud backup, making long-term data management inconvenient. No multi-language support, only adapts to single-language users.

4. Money Lover - Money Manager: Comprehensive functions, supports income/expense tracking, budget management, asset statistics, multiple ledgers, and multiple languages. Rich chart statistics functions can meet advanced bookkeeping needs. However, operations are relatively complex, not friendly enough for ordinary beginners. Some core functions (such as multiple ledger advanced settings) require paid unlocking. There are a few ads in the app.

5. QianJi (Bookkeeping): Adapts to Chinese user habits, clean interface, smooth operation. Supports income/expense recording, budget management, data backup, and CSV export. Privacy protection functions are complete. Core functions are free, no ad interference. However, the design style is monotonous and lacks personalization. Doesn't support multiple languages. Fewer customization options, difficult to meet the needs of international users and users who pursue aesthetics.
